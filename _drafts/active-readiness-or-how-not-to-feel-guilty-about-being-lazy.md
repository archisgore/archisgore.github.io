### Do you:

- find yourself stressed but don’t know how you got there (kind of [like a dream](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J2jEb3hRlWs).) A conversation is going fine, but
- find yourself in “or else…” situations? especially very asymmetric ones? e.g. make me a cup of tea, *or else* die! meet this deadline, *or else* lose your career!
- feel guilty that you could have, should have, would have done more for someone?
- or worse, feel ashamed that someone else said you didn’t do enough? (Shame is very different, and far more dangerous than guilt)
- feel inadequate/incompetent/don’t-measure-up at job/relationships
- feel life is unfair and those who work less get more?
- other people get promoted/valued for **committing to nothing**? (remember that phrase.. we’ll break it down later)
- every attempt to fix it only begets criticism, hate, pushback — you try to work EVEN HARDER and you get told not to, but then how do you win against the person who is already winning by doing nothing?
- find that psychopaths who don’t show any degree of caring, find success?

If you relate/empathize/feel any of the above I’m going to introduce you to a concept of “Active Readiness”, a framework I use to handle all the above.

As credentials, I’ve been in and seen some situations I’m not proud of. I’ve caused many of them. Ever seen this movie moments where someone [shouldn’t have read from a book](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6J-PhFYNbOU), or deployed a build and the consequences were worse than delaying by a day or even a week? I’ve lived those.

Axioms (Fundamental Truths):

- No deader than dead.
- No more alive than alive.
- You cannot will resources into existence.
- Capacity is situational (it can change day to day)
