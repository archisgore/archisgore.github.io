Another year, another cyber incident with hyperbole.

Instead of discussing the flaw is, which has been discussed ad nauseum already, I want to talk about how it affected us and more importantly so, why this keeps happening. This post is not about why the bugs keep happening, or vulnerabilities keep getting disclosed.

This post is about why we keep having to pay a high price every time a new disclosure happens.

*I have a post coming up on exploring the* [*Moral Hazard*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moral_hazard) *this created. Responsible disclosure prompted the teams discovering the flaw to allow vendors time to patch and protect their customers. It also meant however, that for six months, NEW VULNERABLE SURFACE AREA was created by people who had the immediate choice to buy AMD chips instead of Intels. It meant individual people running forked kernels, or custom one-off devices didn’t know they had to fix. It meant that individual users were denied the agency to look for other options than simply waiting for the patch.*

#### We get bullied by press releases

If you follow cybersecurity with any : “Patch!”
