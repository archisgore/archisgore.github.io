If you find yourself confused about decisions. No clear path to go. If you find yourself confused about what to do next? How do you know you’re not giving up something better? How long should you wait? You have an ambitious goal, but how and where should you start?

This is not an uncommon situation to find oneself in. We criticize people for not taking the first steps, but most motivation talks/blogs don’t explain how to know what the damned first step is? This is the addendum blog post they all missed.

You will also learn how to counteract nitpicking. Sometimes from others, but mostly in your own head. You’ll do it using sound science, and knowing that by NOT nitpicking you’re actually getting a more safer/conservative outcome. If this feels a bit counter-intuitive, by the end you’ll be a believer.

You’ll also learn to take shots in the dark comfortably. Turns out, taking shots in the dark is not only necessary, but a fundamental starting point of for learning to happen.

As an entirely fun side-effect, you’ll learn how AI/Deep Learning works.

### Contextualize

The first step to getting unstuck, is knowing the situation. Don’t ever get tempted to “solving” the problem. I have three steps to contextualize.

#### Step 1: Separate Observations, Facts, and Assumptions

Make a list of things you’ve seen, heard, or touched. These are called **observations**.

Make a list of promises or contracts made to you. These are facts. Every fact must have an accountable backer taking responsibility for it.

My car manufacturer taking legal liability for my car is a **fact**. My friend telling me the car is reliable is an **observation**.

Everything else, is an **assumption**.

This is called sanitization of input. If I was working on something new for instance, I would be intrigued by observations, but would only make decisions based on **facts**.

What if you don’t have many facts… what if nobody is saying anything? This leads us to step 2….

**Step 2: Bound the situation; overgeneralize**

This is my favorite thing in the world, and the counterintuitive part. This is a huge theme in my talks on Cyber Security as well. When in doubt, we feel a lack of control or agency. So we do the intuitive thing — micromanage, nitpick, argue, ponder, wonder, etc.

You’ll find that a LOT of situations where we’re totally uncertain, have quite a lot of certainty to them.
